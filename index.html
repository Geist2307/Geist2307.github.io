From Physics to Data Science

TL;DR:

I'm a physicist-turned-data-scientist with a strong foundation in practical machine learning. During my MSc at the University St Andrews, I developed practical Bayesian Neural Networks, which can be made 50-90% more compact with no loss of accuracy. Passionate about turning data into actionable insights .

My journey into science began early on, as I distinguished myself in Romania's National Physics Olympiads during my high school years. .From grade 9 to grade 12, I consistenly ranked in the top 10 students of my year of study in the National Physics Olympiads. In 2012, and then again in 2013, I qualified in the top 20 students of all years that could represent Romania internationally. This means I was very fortunate to compete with the top 0.25% of students of my generation in Physics.

After high school, I took a gap year to travel and volunteer before earning my integrated Masters (MPhys) degree in Physics, from the University of Southampton in 2019, achieving an overal result of Second Class Honours - Upper Division (2:1). For my final-year project I collaborated with the Optoelectronics Research Centre(ORC). I conducted experiments and analyzed time-series data to investigate whether using Hollow-Core fibres could enhance phase stability in Quantum Repeater Architectures. I obtained first-class honours for this project (71%). Physics prepared me to come up with hypothesises, design experiments, collect data and draw conclusions. Skills that I find crucial for a data scientist.

Following my MPhys, I decided to take on a personal academic challenge: Semiotics. I graduated with an MA Semiotics degree from the University of Tartu in 2022, achieving an overall grade B. While not directly related to my Physics background, the programme helped me develop skills in critical thinking, argumentation, and writing. Communication remains a key skill for me in my day to day work, as I communicate insights and limitations to non-tehnical stakeholders.

In August 2022, I joined the University of Tartu's Physics department as a Junior Research Fellow in Physics (Computational Imaging) . My responsabilities included optical experiments - improving the I-COACH and FINCH holographic techniques - and algorithm development ,namely the Lucy-Richardson-Rosen Algorithm, a deconvolution algorithm for image reconstruction. I contributed to several projects, and I was first author on papers published Optics Express and Results in Physics. In addition to research, I was also involved in teaching and communicating science. Some activities included securing a French Government bursary to visit the University of Bordeaux to introduce the FINCH technique to Prof. Etienne Brasselet's SINGULAR group,and presenting some of my work publicly at the 2023 SPIE Photonics Europe conference in Prague. In addition, I co-organized the "CIPHering with CIPHR" Hackathon in September 2022. This was a 48-hour Hackathon meant to introduce students to the field of Computational Imaging. As part of this Hackathon, I gave a workshop on the basics of image processing in MATLAB, and I designed some practical problems through which students could learn about our work. Looking back this was another opportunity to popularize science in a clear and accesible way to non-technical audiences. During my time at Tartu, I also explored directions in AI. In my spare time, I implemented GANs and AutoEncoders for image denoising.

By July 2023, recognizing the strong overlap between Computational Imaging and AI, I fully transitioned into data science. I completed an MSc in Data Science at the University of St Andrews -- a significant milestone, graduating with Distinction (the highest classification). The program emphasizes hands-on, industry-relevant experience, with assignments requiring end-to-end workflows for machine learning and deep learning using tools like PyTorch, PySpark, and Scikit-Learn. In my MSc Dissertation I developed practical Bayesian Neural Networks, that thanks to their solid mathematical foundation can be made up to 90\% smaller than classicial neural networks and thus easier to deploy, for example on Edge devices. I was placed on the Dean's list for academic excellence twice, in 2023/2024 and 2024/2025, having achieved over 90\% in the taught component. With a mix of data fluency, curiosity, adaptabiltiy and passion for communication, I am always eager to cut through to noise and deliver solutions.
